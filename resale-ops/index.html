China-sourced bottleneck arbitrage

Small replacement parts, tested with micro-batches and ruthless ROI rules.

Pilot milestones

The investor decision is made from sell-through, profit, returns, and reorder confidence.

1 Weeks 1-2: scan Compare country price gaps, supplier costs, competitor count, and delivery promises.
2 Weeks 3-5: sample Order from 3-5 suppliers and reject parts with poor fit, packaging, or IP risk.
3 Weeks 6-8: list Receive micro-bulk stock, photograph, list 3-5 SKUs, and run light ad tests.
4 Weeks 9-12: decide Scale, reorder, watch, or liquidate based on profit, sell-through, and returns.
Investor controls

Risk boundaries

This pitch is built around evidence gathering, not guaranteed returns.

Default market US eBay first. Australia, UK, Singapore, and Mercado Livre remain comparison markets.
Fulfillment Micro-bulk stock held domestically. No retail dropshipping from China to buyers.
Product lane Non-safety repair micro-parts with exact-search demand and low shipping weight.
Excluded categories No safety-critical parts, replicas, branded counterfeits, batteries, medical, food, cosmetics, or children’s products.

Pilot assumptions are for planning only and are not a guarantee of investor return. Live supplier quotes, marketplace fees, import costs, and sell-through data must replace seed assumptions before scale-up.
