Hong Kong licence setup

Your first property-agent licence path.

Start with a Salesperson's Licence if you want to join an agency and begin doing estate agency work. Use the Estate Agent's Licence route only if you want the higher licence or plan to control/run an agency business.

Minimum age18+
EducationForm 5 or equivalent
Exam validityApply within 12 months
Best first routeSQE + Salesperson's Licence
Estate agency work in Hong Kong normally requires a valid EAA licence. The EAA states that unlicensed practice can be an offence. If you prefer a marketing-agency model, keep your work to advertising/content/lead systems for licensed agents and avoid handling property introductions, viewings, negotiations, or transaction documents.

Choose Your Route

The salesperson route is the practical entry point. The estate agent route gives more authority but has a broader exam.

Salesperson's Licence

Use this if you want to work for a licensed estate agency and learn listings, viewings, tenancy, and sale workflows.

Recommended first SQE exam Licence: HK$1,280 / 12 months

Estate Agent's Licence (Individual)

Use this if you want the higher licence, may manage/control agency work, or later apply for business particulars.

Higher licence EAQE exam Licence: HK$2,010 / 12 months

Setup Checklist

Tick items as you complete them. Progress is saved in this browser.

0/8

Confirm education proof

Prepare Form 5 or equivalent proof, such as school certificate or accepted examination/qualification evidence.

Before exam

Pick SQE or EAQE

Choose SQE for salesperson only. Choose EAQE if you want eligibility for either estate agent or salesperson licence.

Decision

Register with PEAK Exam Centre

Registration is first come, first served. Computer-based SQE registration is online only.

Exam booking

Study the EAA materials

Read the exam handbook, syllabus, Code of Ethics, practice circulars, and EAA study guide sections.

2-6 weeks

Pass the qualifying exam

Keep the result slip. The licence application must be made within 12 months of the result slip date.

Exam day

Prepare licence application documents

Prepare ID, education proof, result slip, application form, declarations, photo, and the correct fee.

After pass

Submit the EAA licence application

Apply online where eligible, or submit the completed application with supporting documents and payment.

Within 12 months

Join an agency and start compliant work

For salesperson route, work under a licensed estate agent. Do not market yourself as licensed until the licence is granted.

Practical next move

If you are starting from zero, prepare for the next available SQE seat, then apply for the Salesperson's Licence. If you want the higher route, watch the EAQE registration window and make sure you have enough study time.

First target: SQE Then licence application Then agency interview

Business note

If you later open your own estate agency business, an individual licence alone is not enough. You will also need the correct business setup and EAA statement of particulars of business for each place of business and business name.
